Listening and Discernment

Your journey through 10 seasons

"Speak, Lord, for Your servant is listening."

— 1 Samuel 3:10

God often speaks in quiet ways that can easily be missed in the noise and hurry of everyday life. Sabbath creates space to slow down, listen, and become more attentive to His presence and guidance.

This season is not about forcing spiritual experiences or searching for dramatic revelations. Instead, it is about cultivating a posture of attentiveness, humility, and openness before God. Discernment usually grows gradually — through Scripture, prayer, peace, wisdom, and attentiveness.

As you move through each Sabbath Day

  • Quiet distractions
  • Listen prayerfully
  • Pay attention to your inner life
  • Remain open to God's gentle leading
🌅

Before

Prepare your heart to listen with openness and humility before your Sabbath begins.

🌿

During

Practice rhythms of silence, attentiveness, reflection, and listening prayer throughout the day.

🌙

After

Reflect on what you noticed, sensed, or learned during your Sabbath and record your insights.

36
Psalm 46
🌅 Before ▼
  • Reduce unnecessary stimulation before your Sabbath begins.
  • Release mental clutter and distractions as best you can.
Prayer
"Lord, quiet the noise within me."
🌿 During ▼
  • Read Psalm 46 slowly.
  • Spend several periods in silence throughout the day.
  • Notice how often your mind becomes restless or distracted.
  • Gently return your attention to God without judgment.
  • Sit quietly outdoors or in a peaceful place if possible.
  • Allow stillness to become a form of prayer.
🌙 After ▼
What kinds of inner noise surfaced today?
What helped me become more attentive?
  • Thank God for meeting you in quietness.
  • End your Sabbath peacefully.
