Resting in God

Season 8: Listening and Discernment

Creating Space to Hear God More Attentively

"Speak, Lord, for Your servant is listening." — 1 Samuel 3:10

God often speaks in quiet ways that can easily be missed in the noise and hurry of everyday life. Sabbath creates space to slow down, listen, and become more attentive to His presence and guidance.

How to Use These Guidelines

This season is about cultivating a posture of attentiveness, humility, and openness. As you move through these Sabbath Days:

  • Quiet distractions
  • Listen prayerfully
  • Pay attention to your inner life
  • Remain open to God's gentle leading

BEFORE

Prepare your heart to listen with openness and humility.

DURING

Practice rhythms of silence, attentiveness, reflection, and listening prayer.

AFTER

Reflect on what you noticed, sensed, or learned during your Sabbath.

Before

  • Reduce unnecessary stimulation before your Sabbath begins.
  • Pray: "Lord, quiet the noise within me."
  • Release mental clutter and distractions as best you can.

During

  • Read Psalm 46 slowly.
  • Spend several periods in silence throughout the day.
  • Notice mind restlessness without judgment; return to God.
  • Sit quietly outdoors or in a peaceful place if possible.

After

  • Reflect: What kinds of inner noise surfaced today?
  • Reflect: What helped me become more attentive?
  • Thank God for meeting you in quietness.
